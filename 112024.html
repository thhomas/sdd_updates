Stat Of The Week

 
 

This World Diabetes Day, we draw attention to the serious health challenges facing the Pacific region. In 2019, nearly 1 in 3 deaths in Samoa 🇼🇸 were attributed to cardiovascular diseases, cancer, diabetes, or chronic respiratory diseases. This shows the critical need for effective health interventions and increased awareness surrounding non-communicable diseases (NCDs) in Samoa and the Pacific. For more insights on health statistics in the Pacific, visit the Pacific Data Hub and our Population Dashboard. Explore the full dataset here. More info

 

SPC's Ocean Science Technicians with partners from Fiji Metrological Services collect topography points and extract data from pressure sensors or tide wave recorders. This World Tsunami Awareness Day, 5 November, we shine a light on the vulnerability of Pacific populations to tsunamis. In 2021, 40% of people in the Pacific lived within 5 km of the coast, putting them at significant risk from tsunami events.  The Pacific region is particularly susceptible to these hazards due to its location along the Ring of Fire, where tectonic plates frequently shift, leading to earthquakes and subsequent tsunamis.  In contrast, about 41% of the European Union's population lives within 50 km of the coast—though the risk of tsunamis is far lower in Europe due to geographical factors (European Commission, 2020)​.These statistics highlight the need for robust disaster preparedness, especially in high-risk areas. Let’s continue raising awareness and building resilience for coastal communities.  Explore more insights through the Pacific Data Hub.Discover the full dataset here.  More info
